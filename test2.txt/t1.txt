Round robin scheduling uses a fixed time quantum for each process.
Each process gets the CPU for one time slice (quantum) in turn.
If the process does not finish in its quantum, it is preempted and placed at the end of the ready queue.
This repeats until all processes complete.

Example:
- Time quantum = 4 units
- Processes: P1, P2, P3
- CPU order: P1 -> P2 -> P3 -> P1 -> ...

The time quantum should be chosen to balance context switch overhead and response time.
